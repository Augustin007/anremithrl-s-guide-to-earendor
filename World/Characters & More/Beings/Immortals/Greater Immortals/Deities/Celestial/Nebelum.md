### Nebelum

---

## **Lineage:** Elder God of Logic, Knowledge, and the Forge of Mind  
**Birth:** Early Age of the Gods  
**Death:** None — withdrew from Arvandor after the Sundering War  
**Titles:** The Lawful Mind, the Father of Thought, Lord of Measures, The Last Witness, Keeper of the Silent Flame

#### The First Spark of Thought

In the first stirrings after the **Age of the Primordials**, when creation’s raw fold still screamed with heat and motion, there arose among the younger gods a being whose essence was not fire nor chaos but **definition**.  
That being was **Nebelum**, the Mind Awakened, said to have been born from the first equation spoken by the cosmos — the utterance that distinguished _is_ from _is not._

He was not formed in passion or wrath as his siblings were; his birth was the quiet condensation of thought itself. In the earliest days he wandered the new heavens, charting orbits and assigning measures, defining light, length, and time.  
Where Asmodeus bound souls and Tharizdun tore them, Nebelum **named** them — not with chains but with understanding. From him came the first logic, the first language, and the sacred concept of _truth independent of will._

---

#### The Forge of Knowledge

During the **Age of Awakening**, Nebelum watched as the younger gods delighted in their new toys of life and death. He did not interfere in their games; instead, he studied the principles underlying them.  
From his mind flowed the axioms that would become the **Codices of Order**, the seven volumes that defined arithmetic, measure, and geometry in divine form.

He forged the dwarves and gnomes alongside Geb, shaping them as embodiments of curiosity and craft. To the dwarves he gave **Endurance**, to the gnomes **Wonder**, that together they might balance one another.  
Vindel, the mortal mathematician who first recorded fractions, invoked Nebelum’s name before every computation — and claimed the god whispered zero into his ear.

Nebelum was the first to call thought sacred. To him, understanding was worship. His temples were not of gold but of proof and theorem, and his clerics carried styluses instead of swords.

---

#### The Philosopher-God

In the **Age of Building**, when mortals learned language, number, and philosophy, Nebelum’s influence flowered.  
Aelfwin’s _First Order Logic_ and Dallion’s _Ahiarime_ were both inspired by dreams sent from Nebelum’s court. He walked unseen among scholars, sometimes appearing as a stooped librarian, sometimes as a child muttering equations into the dust.

He debated Thalorin endlessly over the nature of free will, concluding,

> “The mortal’s mind is the universe striving to understand itself.”

While other gods sought worship, Nebelum sought **proof** — not belief, but comprehension. Those who prayed to him found their thoughts clarified, their arguments sharpened, their doubts laid bare.  
It is said that the first abacuses, the first constellations mapped, the first concept of causality — all were born from his silent prompting.

---

#### The God Who Voted for Mortals

When Araushnee’s crime shattered Arvandor and the council debated whether to **unmake mortals**, Nebelum sat silent through all their quarrels.  
Asmodeus spoke of flaw, Thalorin of mercy.  
The vote stood even — until Nebelum, calm and weary, raised his hand for the mortals.

> “If perfection requires ignorance,” he said, “then imperfection is the only moral truth.”

That single gesture preserved creation. It was he who declared the decree: _The council shall not act as one to unmake life._  
From that moment, Asmodeus and Tharizdun called him _traitor_; Thalorin named him _brother._

When the **Sundering War** erupted, Nebelum joined the Protectors, crafting wards and measures that held the Weave together. His runes of containment bound storms and gods alike. But each victory came at cost: for every law he wrote, chaos found a loophole. At the war’s end he was exhausted, his light dimmed.

---

#### The Withdrawal

After the Betrayer Gods were sealed, Nebelum did not celebrate. He saw that even righteousness had been written in blood and paradox. He gathered his tablets of law, erased their titles, and cast them into the firmament, where they became the **Seven Stars of Measure** — still visible in northern skies.

He withdrew from Arvandor, declaring that the gods had failed their own logic. In his stead he left behind the **Attendants of Knowledge**, an order of luminous beings who carried fragments of his mind into the mortal world.  
Lorekeep, founded millennia later by Theriad, stands upon one of the last shrines built in Nebelum’s honor.

His name became a benediction among scholars, engineers, and artificers:

> “By Nebelum’s Balance,” they say, when something elegant holds.

---

#### The Modern Era

Through the ages, Nebelum has not died but **quieted**. His essence flickers in those who seek truth without reward — the mathematician who proves a theorem no one will read, the historian who preserves a record no one will remember.

In the **Age of Harmony**, his influence was rekindled in the universities of Veloria and Strixhaven. Tal’berius Tar’rilion himself was called “Nebelum’s heir,” for his work in the Codex Arcanum mirrored the god’s own union of logic and magic. The Attendants of Knowledge in Veloria claim to receive dreams still — faint, crystalline whispers: equations carved in starlight.

During the Shattering, when Asmodeus regained divinity and Auril was slain, Nebelum’s presence was noted one last time. As the Tree of Names fell, Colossus Methunroa heard a voice in the collapse:

> “Remember the first proof: nothing true can be destroyed.”

Some scholars believe Nebelum merged with the Conduit — that the laws stabilizing the planes are his final theorem, endlessly calculating the equilibrium between creation and void.

- **Symbols:** A seven-pointed star divided by a line; a balance scale overlaid on a circle of runes; an unblinking eye of silver.
    
- **Temples:** Lorekeep (Veloria), the Observatory of Measure (Hatreth), the Archive of Ahiarime (Kesna)
    
- **Seat:** The Hall of Ratios — a floating citadel of glass and brass orbiting the Silver Star Nebel, visible only during eclipses.
    
- **Current Status:** Withdrawn from divine politics. Worship persists among the Attendants of Knowledge and among mathematicians who view him as patron of pure thought.

### **The Creed of Nebelum**

> _To think is to forge._  
> _To measure is to remember._  
> _Truth is the only flame that does not burn._  
> _All error is holy, for it reveals the limits of reason._
